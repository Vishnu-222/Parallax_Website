BIKING

Biking is a fantastic activity that encompasses various forms and purposes, from leisurely rides through the park to intense mountain biking adventures.Biking is an excellent form of exercise, offering cardiovascular benefits, improved joint mobility, muscle strength, and mental well-being.Biking is eco-friendly, emitting no greenhouse gases or air pollutants, making it a sustainable mode of transportation.

Biking often fosters a sense of community, with group rides, races, and events held worldwide. These events range from local charity rides to international competitions like the Tour de France.Wearing a helmet is crucial for safety. Additionally, following traffic rules, using lights at night, and being aware of road conditions can help prevent accidents.

PARA GLIDING

Paragliding is an exhilarating sport that involves flying through the air using a fabric wing (paraglider) and harness.Paragliders typically launch from elevated locations such as hillsides or mountains.Once airborne, pilots use controls attached to the harness to manipulate the shape of the wing and control their direction, speed, and altitude. Steering is achieved by shifting body weight and pulling on control lines.

Pilots typically land on designated landing zones. Landing techniques vary depending on wind conditions and terrain. It's essential to approach the landing area with the correct speed and angle to ensure a safe touchdown.Paragliding offers a unique perspective of the world from above, combining the thrill of flight with the tranquility of soaring among the clouds. It's a sport that rewards skill, experience, and a deep appreciation for the beauty of nature.

SURFING

Surfing is a dynamic water sport that involves riding waves while standing or lying on a surfboard. Originating in ancient Polynesian cultures, it has evolved into a popular global activity. Another prominent form of surfing is body boarding, where a surfer rides the wave on a bodyboard, either lying on their belly, drop knee (one foot and one knee on the board), or sometimes even standing up on a body board.

Other types of surfing include knee boarding, surf matting (riding inflatable mats) and using foils. Body surfing, in which the wave is caught and ridden using the surfer's own body rather than a board, is very common and is considered by some surfers to be the purest form of surfing. The closest form of body surfing using a board is a handboard which normally has one strap over it to fit on one hand.
